Overview

Transit Ops Lab is an interactive dashboard for monitoring reliability, cost, and emissions across city bus routes. The dashboard shows key performance indicators (KPIs) and a set of charts that update when you change the selected route or metric. All charts appear on a single Summary view; you can show or hide individual charts and open any chart in a larger, resizable modal.

Toolbar

At the top of the dashboard, two dropdowns control what data is shown:

Route
Select one of the six routes (Route A through F). All charts that depend on a single route (e.g., histogram, line chart, emissions, forecast) update to show data for that route.
Metric
Choose Ridership or On-time %. This affects the histogram, line chart, and any chart that displays “the selected metric.”

KPI Cards

Below the toolbar, four cards show aggregate values when pipeline data is available:

  • Total ridership — Sum of ridership across all routes.
  • Total cost — Total cost in USD.
  • Total CO₂ (kg) — Total tailpipe CO₂.
  • Routes — Number of routes in the dataset.

If you have not run the pipeline, these cards may be empty; the rest of the dashboard still works with the built-in route CSVs.

Chart Grid

The main area is a two-column grid of up to 10 charts:

  • Histogram, Line Chart, Time Series, Scatterplot
  • Emissions over time, Total cost by route, Vehicle type usage
  • Ridership by route, Forecast, Data Quality

Showing and hiding charts

Each chart card has a Show checkbox in the header. Uncheck it to hide that chart; the card disappears from the grid so the layout reflows. Hidden charts are listed as buttons in the restore bar above the grid (e.g., “Show Histogram”). Click a button to bring that chart back and check its Show box again.

Time Series Chart

The Time series chart plots both Ridership and On-time % over time (with two y-axes). The legend shows “Variables” with two items: Ridership and On-time %. Click a legend item to toggle that series on or off. At least one variable remains visible. This helps you focus on one series or compare both.

Chart Modal (Zoom View)

Click anywhere on a chart’s plot area (not the “Show” checkbox) to open it in a larger modal window.

What you can do in the modal

  • Pan — Drag the chart to move it.
  • Zoom — Use the mouse wheel (or pinch on touch devices) to zoom in and out (about 0.4× to 8×).
  • Resize the window — Drag the bottom-right corner of the modal (the resize handle) to make the window larger or smaller.

Close the modal by clicking the × button, clicking the dark backdrop outside the chart, or pressing Escape. The Data Quality chart opens in the same modal but does not support pan/zoom (it is a table).

Keyboard & Accessibility

  • Escape — Closes the chart modal when it is open.
  • When the modal opens, focus moves to the close button so you can press Enter or Space to close it.
  • Dropdowns and buttons are keyboard accessible; use Tab to move between controls.
